👖 Indigo-Optimizer AI: Multimodal Digital Twin
Autonomous Chemical Stabilization & EU-Compliance Automation
📌 Executive Summary
Indigo-Optimizer AI is a professional-grade Digital Twin designed for the high-speed denim manufacturing sector in Karachi. It solves the three most expensive problems in a dye house: chemical wastage, inconsistent fabric shading, and manual compliance reporting. By fusing Computer Vision with PID Control Theory, this system ensures that every meter of fabric meets international buyer standards (Levi's, H&M, Zara) while reducing the chemical footprint by up to 12%.

🛠️ The "Agentic" Architecture
The system consists of four specialized AI Agents working in a synchronized "Closed-Loop":

The Chemist (PID Controller): Replaces "guesswork" dosing with high-precision mathematics. It looks at the past, present, and predicted future drift of the Redox potential to stabilize the indigo bath.

The Shade Guardian (Vision AI): A real-time OpenCV inspector that monitors fabric intensity. If a shade starts to fade, it automatically "overrides" the chemical pumps to correct the color before the fabric leaves the vat.

The Maintenance Engineer (PdM): Analyzes pump vibration patterns to predict mechanical failure before it stops production, saving hours of expensive downtime.

The Compliance Auditor (DPP): Automatically logs every chemical addition into a secure ledger, generating an EU-compliant Digital Product Passport (DPP) for every batch.

📈 Business Impact (The "ROI")
Reduced Rework: Eliminates "B-Grade" fabric by catching shade variations in milliseconds.

Export Ready: Automates the sustainability documentation required for the EU Green Deal 2026.

Chemical Savings: High-precision PID dosing prevents the over-saturation of Sodium Hydrosulphite.

Zero Downtime: Predictive alerts allow maintenance during scheduled breaks rather than emergency stops.

💻 Tech Stack
Language: Python 3.x

Intelligence: PID Control Theory, Stochastic Modeling.

Vision: OpenCV (Multimodal intensity analysis).

Data: Pandas/NumPy for time-series sensor simulation.
